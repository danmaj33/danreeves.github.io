How The Clouds Role

By Daniel Reeves


There is no such thing as the middle of nowhere. Everywhere is somewhere to sombody. As you stand at the intersection of a country road and nowhere, you begin to feel time. Time in those moments seems to freeze in the summer heat. Layered like a lazagne with years upon years of nothing rolling by. This is cloud counrty, the golden grasses dance under the cotton candy puff that floats overhead, shades of grays, whites, purple, and blue swirl. This spot is boring, it is timeless, it may be the most interesting place on earth not for what may have happend but what did happen. What happened here you ask. I respond by whispering "time rolled".
