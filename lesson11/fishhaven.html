Time Stands

By Daniel Reeves


It's so hot. The heat waves back as I stare down the bleached black top streaked with yellow. I asked to be here. So I sit and I wait. I stare at the golden rug of layout before me, ripple and sway. I think about how many times I have been passed this way. How much time I have past this way. The old cabin sits in the center of the rug. It has been there for years, decades, more. Nothing has changed for so long. It will be the same when I return.
